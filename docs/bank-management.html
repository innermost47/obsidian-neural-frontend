Bank Management

Learn how to manage, organize, preview, and drag samples from the Bank in Obsidian Neural.

Estimated time: 15 minutes

1

Step 1: Opening the Bank

The Bank is where all generated samples are stored. Click on the Bank button located in the bottom bar of the main interface.

Video: Opening the Bank from the main interface

The Bank window will appear, showing all generated samples since you started using Obsidian Neural.

2

Step 2: Sample Categories

You can create new categories to organize your samples by genre, mood, or sound type.

  • Click on Add Category to create a new one
  • Assign a category to any sample in the Bank (e.g. “Ambient”, “Drums”, “Synths”)
  • Rename or delete categories as needed

Video: Managing sample categories in the Bank

Pro Tip

Categories help you organize your creative ideas — for example, group all “Lo-Fi” loops together or separate “AI drums” from “AI basslines”.

3

Step 3: Filtering & Searching

Once your categories are set, you can filter and search through your entire sound bank easily.

  • Use the Filter dropdown to show only samples of a given category
  • Use the Search bar to find samples by name or keyword

Video: Filtering and searching within the Bank

4

Step 4: Cleaning Unused Samples

Over time, you might accumulate unused samples. Use the Clean Unused Samples function to free disk space.

  • Click Clean Unused Samples in the Bank menu
  • This will permanently delete all samples not linked to any project
Warning

This action is irreversible. Make sure to back up important sounds before cleaning.

Screenshot: Cleaning unused samples from the Bank

5

Step 5: Drag & Drop

The Bank supports drag-and-drop both to the plugin and to your DAW:

  • Drag a sample from the Bank onto a track inside Obsidian Neural
  • Hold Ctrl + Click to drag a sample from the Bank directly into your DAW
  • You can also drag from a track to your DAW using Ctrl + Click

Video: Dragging samples

6

Step 6: Preview & Management

You can preview each sample directly in the Bank before using it.

  • Click the Preview button next to any sample
  • Delete unwanted samples directly from the Bank

Video: Previewing samples
